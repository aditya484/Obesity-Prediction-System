Understanding the Metrics

  • BMI (Body Mass Index): Calculated via Weight / Height². Indicates typical mass ranges.
  • Hydration (CH2O): Proper intake crucial for metabolic functions. Aim for >2L daily.
  • Physical Activity (FAF): Moderate to vigorous activity strongly correlates with lower obesity risk.

Obesity Classifications Reference

This AI assessment categorizes physical risk into globally recognized classes based on Body Mass Index (BMI) ranges and correlated lifestyle factors derived from standard medical datasets.

Insufficient Weight Below standard BMI (< 18.5). May indicate malnourishment, metabolic constraints, or rapid unintended weight depletion.
Normal Weight Healthy BMI zone (18.5 - 24.9). This represents the optimal equilibrium for metabolic stability and cardiovascular longevity.
Overweight (Level I & II) Elevated BMI (25.0 - 29.9). The pre-obesity stage. Warrants lifestyle and dietary interventions to halt upward trajectory.
Obesity Type I BMI (30.0 - 34.9). Low-risk clinical obesity. Characterized by moderate excess adiposity. Dietary reform heavily recommended.
Obesity Type II BMI (35.0 - 39.9). Moderate-risk obesity. Strongly correlated with chronic conditions like hypertension, dyslipidemia, and type 2 diabetes.
Obesity Type III BMI (40.0+). Severe or 'morbid' obesity. High-risk category. Often requires aggressive, sustained medical or surgical intervention.
